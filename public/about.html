Made for Louisiana

Public emergency information, gathered with care.

Louisiana911 brings separate official public-safety feeds into one readable map. The project grew from the Caddo 911 monitor and now serves a wider Louisiana audience without erasing its Caddo roots.

Louisiana911 is not an emergency service. Information can be delayed, incomplete, changed, or removed. Never use this site to decide whether to seek help. Call 911 for an emergency.

Location uncertainty stays visible.

Baton Rouge traffic incidents use the approximate point published by the City-Parish traffic map when one is available. If that map layer lags the incident list, Louisiana911 may derive an approximate marker from the published address range or cross-street corridor.

Louisiana911 preserves the public location label supplied by each source. When New Orleans publishes a block or intersection but supplies POINT(0 0) instead of a usable map point, the site may derive an approximate marker from that same public text. Those markers are larger, dashed, and explicitly labeled approximate; the displayed address is never made more specific than NOPD's published label.

What Louisiana911 filters from New Orleans.

The NOPD Calls for Service dataset includes officer activity and routine administrative traffic that is not useful for this crime and public-safety view. Louisiana911 first excludes officer-initiated rows, then omits these generic final call types:

  • Area Check
  • Business Check
  • Complaint Other
  • Disturbance (Other)
  • Fugitive Attachment
  • Incident Requested by Another Agency
  • Medical
  • Mental Patient
  • Return for Additional Info
  • Tow Impounded Vehicle (Private)

If a generic final type began with a non-generic initial classification, Louisiana911 keeps it under that initial classification. This safeguard retains potentially material reports such as theft, battery, burglary, robbery, or protection-order violations while still removing routine city noise.
